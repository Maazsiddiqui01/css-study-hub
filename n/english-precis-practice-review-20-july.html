Précis Practice Clinic: From Rough Draft to Controlled Summary

A line-by-line workshop on extracting the passage's idea, writing a complete title, compressing without distortion, and revising language for clarity.

A strong précis is built through visible revision: find the idea, control the sentence, remove the distraction, then read the whole piece again. ✍️

⭐ The five-minute version

  • Read for the controlling idea before polishing language. Every draft in the workshop is judged first on whether it has understood the passage about organizational citizenship and workplace performance.
  • Make the title directional. A topic label such as Organizational Citizenship Behavior is incomplete. Add the relationship or effect the paragraph develops, such as its role in performance or its positive organizational effects.
  • Keep the source argument intact. The practice passage contrasts formal duties, knowledge and skill with voluntary conduct shaped by motivation, attitude, personality and workplace relationships. Examples support that contrast; they do not replace it.
  • Prefer short, complete sentences. Many drafts know the right points but bury them inside long structures. Split at a completed thought, check the new subject and verb, then reconnect only where the logic is clear.
  • Choose precise words. The teacher repeatedly removes inflated or unsuitable vocabulary, repairs agreement and asks students to use ordinary words accurately.
  • Keep the prose idea-centred. In this course method, first- and second-person pronouns are removed from the précis so attention stays on the passage rather than the writer or reader.
  • Conclude the argument. The final sentence should complete the passage's movement, not stop after another detail. For this exercise, the conclusion returns to organizational performance, culture or management.
  • Practise in public, revise in private. The workshop treats correction as a learning tool. A draft becomes useful when its weaknesses are identified and repaired, not when it is hidden.

2 Passage map

🧭 Map the passage before compressing it

00:12:46 to 00:35:54

Most weak sentences in the workshop begin with a content problem: the writer has copied a phrase, drifted into a side point or lost the relationship the passage is trying to explain. The cure is to map the argument before compressing it.

The practice passage concerns organizational citizenship behavior. Across the repeated readings, the teacher reconstructs its contrast with conventional job performance. Formal performance concerns assigned duties and job-description requirements; citizenship behavior concerns voluntary conduct that helps colleagues and the organization 00:21:23 00:34:38.

Passage elementRecovered pointExamples heard in review
Starting contrastAssigned job performance is distinguished from helpful conduct beyond formal duties.Work listed in a job description versus voluntary assistance 00:35:16.
Basis of formal performanceKnowledge, education, ability and skill are associated with completing assigned work.The teacher repeatedly contrasts these with non-technical qualities 00:25:47 00:55:02.
Basis of citizenship behaviorMotivation, attitude, personality, trust, respect and a sense of obligation shape voluntary conduct.The list is refined in several student drafts 00:25:47 00:35:54.
Concrete conductHelping a colleague, guiding a new employee, supporting extra activities and offering useful suggestions.These examples recur in the teacher's corrections 00:33:42 00:41:26 00:50:41.
Claimed effectCooperation, relationships, efficiency, culture and management may improve.The teacher redirects one draft from “problems” toward the passage's positive effect 00:23:15.

A précis writer does not need every repeated example. The writer needs the relationship: voluntary supportive behavior extends the way employee contribution is understood, and that extension has implications for organizational performance and management. Once that sentence is clear, details can be ranked by whether they support it.

A four-line extraction before drafting

  1. Topic: organizational citizenship behavior.
  2. Contrast: voluntary supportive conduct versus assigned job duties.
  3. Mechanism: attitude, motivation and personality encourage conduct such as helping and advising.
  4. Result: better cooperation, relationships, culture or organizational functioning.

Source-content boundary. This map reports the argument of the classroom passage as reconstructed from the teacher's repeated feedback. It is not an independent research claim about every organization or employee. In a précis, fidelity to the source argument comes before adding outside theory.

Correction: the concept is not newly coined today. The class repeatedly calls organizational citizenship behavior a new or emerging concept 00:20:54 00:29:21. Bateman and Organ were already studying employee “citizenship” in a 1983 Academy of Management Journal article, and a major 2000 review described a large, conceptually contested literature. Research also questions whether every citizenship behavior remains outside the employee's understood role. Keep “new” only as wording from the exercise passage, not as a current historical claim.

The class also demonstrates that several titles may be valid. Different writers notice different legitimate relationships, so titles need not be identical. The two-part title test

  • Noun test: does the title name the central subject?
  • Direction test: does it name the change, role, cause, effect or contrast developed in the passage?

Fast title formula. Write central concept + relationship. For this passage: Organizational Citizenship + Performance, Organizational Citizenship + Positive Effects, or Voluntary Conduct + Organizational Culture. Then choose the version that covers the whole paragraph with the least wording.

Assessment boundary. The teacher sometimes scores the practice body out of ten and the title out of five 00:24:10 00:26:38. Treat that as the workshop's feedback scale. It is not presented in this recording as a universal or current FPSC marking scheme.

4 Compression

✂️ Select and compress without losing the argument

00:20:54 to 00:40:00

Compression is successful when fewer words carry the same argument. A shorter paragraph that drops the controlling relationship is incomplete; a longer paragraph that repeats examples has not yet been compressed.

The teacher praises students who can extract the idea and rewrite it in their own words 00:26:38. He also distinguishes extraction from expansion: one draft has found good points but has not yet reduced the source to the expected proportion 00:37:13. That produces two separate checks:

CheckQuestionTypical failure
Idea fidelityDoes the draft preserve the source's central contrast and result?Introducing a new claim, such as saying the concept creates management problems when the source develops benefits 00:23:15.
SelectionHas the writer kept the essential categories and removed repeated illustrations?Listing every kind of helpful conduct.
ProportionIs the draft genuinely compressed?Paraphrasing each source sentence at almost the same length.
Own wordingHas the writer rebuilt the language?Carrying over a distinctive phrase without need.
ClosureDoes the last sentence complete the argument?Ending on an example or an unfinished consequence.

When one attempt contains 87 words, the teacher tells the student not to obsess over the number during this short exercise. Quality matters, and a difference of roughly ten to fifteen words around the practice target is not treated as the central problem 00:30:13. The broader lesson is sensible: first make the paragraph faithful and coherent, then tighten it to the required limit.

Editorial synthesis of the exercise passage

Possible title: Organizational Citizenship and Performance

Organizational citizenship behavior refers to voluntary workplace conduct beyond assigned duties. It includes assisting colleagues, guiding new employees and offering useful suggestions. Formal job performance draws heavily on knowledge and skills, while citizenship behavior reflects motivation, attitude, personality and a sense of obligation. Such conduct can improve cooperation, workplace relationships and organizational effectiveness. Management therefore gains from considering supportive behavior alongside assigned work.

This paragraph is an editorial reconstruction from the repeated classroom corrections, not a verbatim model dictated by the teacher.

5 Sentence control

🧱 Control the sentence

00:15:57 to 00:47:42

Several students understand the source but lose clarity in the sentence. The teacher's most repeated remedy is to shorten the structure and let each completed thought carry one clear subject-verb relationship.

The first direct instruction is to make sentences short so the idea can be expressed better 00:15:57. The same correction returns when a draft chains definitions, causes and examples into one long sentence 00:29:21. Another draft is stopped three times so one sentence can be divided into two or three completed units 00:32:46.

Overloaded moveRepairWhy it works
Definition + contrast + example + effect in one sentenceGive the definition one sentence, the contrast a second, and the effect a third.Each claim can be checked independently.
A new subject appears after a commaUse a full stop if the first thought is complete.The reader sees where one proposition ends.
Several abstract nouns hide the actionRestore a direct subject and verb.The sentence says who or what does what.
A connector appears without a clear logical relationName the relation first: contrast, addition, cause or result.The connector then performs a real job.

The teacher repeatedly values simple language with clean structure. One draft is praised for an accurate sentence about the changing evaluation of job performance, but its later sentence is called too long and is split 00:33:16 00:34:11. Another writer has strong vocabulary, yet the instruction remains the same: shorten the sentences and improve word choice so the reader can follow the mature content 00:45:13 00:45:43.

The full-stop test

  1. Underline the subject and verb in the sentence.
  2. Circle every point where a new subject begins.
  3. Ask whether the thought before that point is already complete.
  4. If yes, test a full stop. Begin the next sentence directly.
  5. Read both sentences aloud and restore a connector only if the relation needs one.

Précis advantage. Short sentences make revision faster. You can test fidelity, grammar and word count one unit at a time. Complexity should come from the relationship between ideas, not from forcing every relationship into one grammatical structure.

The workshop treats language accuracy as part of meaning. A misplaced word, broken agreement or uncontrolled comma can change the relationship between the source's points.

The teacher's governing principle is accuracy as communication: a writer must be accurate in language to communicate clearly 00:30:48. Sophisticated vocabulary earns praise only when it works. Words such as reciprocate and pursuits attract attention, but the surrounding sentence is still tested for meaning and fit 00:47:42.

Correction: “contribute towards” is not universally ungrammatical. The teacher prefers contribute to in the sentence being edited 00:22:18. That is a concise and natural choice here. Standard English also permits contribute toward/towards in appropriate contexts, so the workshop correction should be treated as a context and economy choice rather than an absolute ban.

A six-pass language scan

  1. Subjects and verbs: agreement and completeness.
  2. Pronouns: clear reference and consistent person.
  3. Connectors: a real logical relation.
  4. Punctuation: sentence boundaries before internal commas.
  5. Word choice: precise, ordinary and economical.
  6. Sound: read aloud once to catch repetition and broken rhythm.
7 Academic voice

🎙️ Keep the voice formal and idea-centred

00:13:57 to 00:53:54

A précis should keep the passage in the foreground. The workshop removes unexplained abbreviations and personal pronouns when they distract from that purpose.

Abbreviations

When a draft uses JD for job description, the teacher questions whether an abbreviation should appear that way 00:13:57. Later, a student shortens organizational citizenship behavior to OCB, and the teacher asks for the full expression rather than an arbitrary shortening 00:26:13.

For a short précis, the economy gained from an abbreviation may be smaller than the clarity lost. Write the full term unless the abbreviation is established, necessary and used enough to justify defining it.

First and second person

The teacher repeatedly crosses out we, our, us and your. In one draft, we feel an obligation is recast impersonally so the sentence remains about the idea 00:31:49. Another draft loses several personal references in rapid succession because they pull the paragraph away from the source 00:41:26. The instruction is repeated near the end: avoid first and second person and stay on the idea 00:53:02 00:53:54.

Personal formIdea-centred revision
We feel an obligation to help.A sense of obligation encourages helpful conduct.
It improves our workplace.Such conduct can improve workplace cooperation.
You can guide new workers.Employees may guide new colleagues.
In this way, we develop trust.This cooperation can develop trust.

Course-rule boundary. First- and second-person pronouns are valid English and appear in many legitimate forms of writing. The transcript teaches their removal as a précis and formal-answer technique, not as a universal grammar rule.

Identity still matters. The teacher also tells a strong writer to maintain individuality in writing 00:48:35. Formal voice does not require mechanical sameness. Writers can differ in title, ordering and diction while preserving the same source meaning.

8 Completion

✅ Finish with coherence and a real conclusion

00:36:19 to 01:01:47

A précis can contain correct sentences and still feel unfinished. The final revision asks whether the paragraph moves as one argument and lands on the source's result.

At 00:51:40, the teacher identifies a draft that has relevant material but no conclusion. The remedy is not a decorative last line. It is a sentence that completes the argument already developed. A later student is praised for closing the paragraph even though the structure still needs shortening 00:55:02. Another receives the same praise for concluding the ideas clearly 00:59:48.

For this passage, the logic should move in this order

1 · DefineVoluntary supportive conduct beyond assigned duties.
→
2 · ContrastFormal skills and duties versus attitude-driven cooperation.
→
3 · IllustrateHelp, guidance and useful suggestions.
→
4 · ConcludeImplications for performance, relationships, culture or management.

The teacher also insists that revision remains active. Writing is described as an activity that improves through more writing 00:53:54. Near the end, the metaphor is to keep “writing mode” switched on because the competitive examination itself is written 00:56:07. The practical meaning is repetition with feedback: each attempt should target the previous attempt's clearest weakness.

The repeatable revision loop

  1. Cover the source. State the core argument from memory in one sentence.
  2. Compare. Reopen the source and restore any essential relationship that was lost.
  3. Title. Make sure it names the subject and direction.
  4. Paragraph. Order definition, contrast, support and result.
  5. Sentences. Split overloaded structures and repair agreement.
  6. Voice. Remove distracting pronouns and unexplained short forms.
  7. Compression. Cut repeated examples and inflated phrases.
  8. Conclusion. Complete the source's movement in the last sentence.

One-error practice. After each draft, name the single highest-value repair: wrong central idea, incomplete title, missing conclusion, sentence overload, pronoun drift, weak punctuation or inaccurate diction. Write the next draft with that error under deliberate control. This converts feedback into a measurable habit.

Exam focus

🎯 Turn the workshop into a marking routine

application

Before drafting

  • Write the central idea in one sentence.
  • List the source's main contrast and final result.
  • Rank examples. Keep only one if the category is already clear.
  • Draft a title using concept + relationship.

While drafting

  • Open with the concept and direction, not a vague claim about the topic being important.
  • Move through definition, contrast, one compact illustration and result.
  • Stop a sentence when its thought is complete.
  • Use ordinary vocabulary accurately. Do not add a difficult word merely to sound formal.
  • Keep the source in the foreground and avoid personal commentary.

Common mistakes exposed by the class

  • A topic-only title with no direction.
  • A long sentence that contains several different propositions.
  • A new claim that reverses the passage's stated effect.
  • Unexplained abbreviations such as JD or OCB in a very short answer.
  • Personal pronouns that shift attention away from the source.
  • Strong vocabulary placed in a sentence it does not fit.
  • A paragraph that stops after a detail and never concludes.
  • Word-count anxiety before fidelity and structure are under control.
