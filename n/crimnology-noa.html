⭐ The five-minute version

  • INTERPOL enables global police cooperation. Its 196 member countries exchange criminal data and intelligence through National Central Bureaus, coordinate cross-border work and help locate fugitives. It does not operate as a supranational police force with its own general arrest power.
  • Colour-coded notices have distinct functions. The class covers Red, Green, Yellow, Black and Orange Notices. They concern wanted persons, warnings about possible public-safety threats, missing persons, unidentified bodies and serious imminent threats.
  • A Red Notice does not itself convict, extradite or compel an arrest. It is a request to locate and provisionally arrest a wanted person pending extradition or similar lawful action, with action governed by the receiving country's law.
  • Pakistan's link is its National Central Bureau in Islamabad. The NCB connects national law enforcement with INTERPOL's network. Pakistan became a member in 1952, correcting the lecture's stated year of 1948.
  • Europol and the IPA are not interchangeable. Europol is the European Union's law-enforcement cooperation agency. The International Police Association is a friendship and professional network for police personnel and does not investigate crimes.
  • The UN-related bodies have different mandates. UNAFEI develops criminal-justice capacity and knowledge; UNODC works across drugs, crime, corruption and related conventions; UNICEF connects through child protection, trafficking, child-sensitive justice, diversion and rehabilitation.
  • Past-paper answers begin with three filters. Identify the demand, identify the stakeholders named or implied, and build a focused outline. The teacher requires repeated five-minute outlining practice before full answer writing.

2 The global coordination body

🌐 INTERPOL: purpose, powers and cooperation

00:00-08:42

The lecture introduces INTERPOL through its scale and purpose: it began in 1923, is headquartered in Lyon and connects 196 member countries 00:00:00. Its value lies in cooperation across borders, especially where one state's police cannot resolve a transnational problem alone.

Correction: formal identity. The body is the International Criminal Police Organization - INTERPOL, an intergovernmental organization. It traces its origin to the International Criminal Police Commission founded in 1923. Its current headquarters are in Lyon, France, and its current membership is 196 countries.

What INTERPOL is meant to achieve

Objective from the lecturePractical meaning
Promote international police cooperationEnable national police bodies to work together across borders 00:01:06.
Combat transnational and organized crimeCoordinate where offending, offenders, victims, money or evidence cross jurisdictions 00:02:23.
Exchange criminal intelligenceLet member countries share information that can support prevention, identification and investigation 00:04:12.
Locate fugitivesHelp a requesting country trace a wanted person who has moved into another member country 00:05:22.
Coordinate assistanceConnect the relevant national authorities so they can act under their own laws and procedures.

The essential limit

INTERPOL does not replace domestic police. The lecturer expressly warns that it is not a police force with ordinary arresting powers 00:00:34. A country communicates through the network, but the competent national authority carries out any search, detention, arrest or extradition permitted by its law.

Do not infer the agency from the word "international". During a classroom aside, the teacher corrects an attempt to attribute a United States-led intelligence operation to INTERPOL 00:05:22. The exam lesson is wider: identify the mandate and responsible institution before using an example.

A precise definition. INTERPOL is a global intergovernmental police-cooperation organization that enables member-country authorities to exchange data, issue alerts and coordinate cross-border action while legal enforcement remains with national authorities.

3 Shared alerts and requests

🚨 INTERPOL's colour-coded notices

08:09-18:32

The best-known cooperation mechanism discussed is the colour-coded notice system 00:08:09. A notice lets member-country police share an alert or request for information. The class concentrates on five colours.

NoticeFunction taught and clarifiedMemory cue
RedSeeks the location and provisional arrest of a wanted person pending extradition, surrender or similar lawful action 00:09:13.Wanted person
GreenWarns about a person's criminal activities where that person is considered a possible threat to public safety 00:12:20.Public-safety warning
YellowHelps locate a missing person, often a minor, or identify someone unable to identify themselves 00:12:45.Missing or unidentified living person
BlackSeeks information about an unidentified body 00:13:24.Unidentified body
OrangeWarns of an event, person, object or process that represents a serious and imminent threat to public safety 00:14:33.Imminent threat

Correction: a Red Notice is not an international arrest warrant. The lecture compresses the sequence into "locate, arrest and extradite" 00:10:36. The notice is a request for cooperation. It does not compel a member country to arrest or extradite, and each country applies its own law.

Green and Orange: the thin line

The lecturer distinguishes these by scope 00:15:07. Green centres on a person's criminal activities and the risk that person may pose. Orange can concern a person, object, event or process presenting a serious and imminent public-safety threat. Both communicate warnings, but Orange is not limited to an offender.

Are notices public?

The teacher warns students not to infer from rumours whether a named person was subject to a notice, describing this information as confidential 00:17:48. That caution is sound for exam writing: do not turn an assumption into a case example.

Correction: public access is limited, not impossible. Most INTERPOL notices are for police use only, but an extract can be made public when the requesting country seeks public assistance. A search result or media claim is still not enough to establish the full legal status or circumstances of a case.

How to earn analysis marks. After defining a notice, connect it with the larger purpose: notices convert transnational information-sharing into a structured alert that national authorities can assess and act upon.

5 Regional agency and professional network

🤝 Europol and the International Police Association

30:16-40:15

Europol

The lecture presents Europol as the European Union's regional law-enforcement cooperation body, headquartered in The Hague, Netherlands 00:30:16. It supports the EU's 27 member states on serious and organized crime, terrorism, drug and human trafficking, cybercrime and related investigations 00:31:34.

Correction: date and powers. The lecture says Europol was made in 1998 and describes an on-ground police contribution 00:30:56. Europol became fully operational on 1 July 1999. It supports investigations initiated by member states, but Europol officers do not independently arrest citizens or initiate investigations.

The teacher's larger comparison remains useful: Europol is regional, while INTERPOL is global. Europol's primary mandate concerns the EU, but it also cooperates with non-EU partner states and international organizations when the problem extends beyond Europe 00:34:21.

The International Police Association

The IPA brings serving and retired police personnel into an international professional and friendship network 00:34:56. The lecture associates it with:

  • training and capacity building;
  • seminars, conferences and professional programs;
  • exchange of ethical-policing ideas and best practices;
  • knowledge from experienced and retired officers;
  • research and exposure to modern policing techniques 00:38:12.

Correction: association, not investigative body. The exact name is the International Police Association, founded by Arthur Troop on 1 January 1950. Its own description calls it a friendship organization and states that it does not investigate crimes. Use it for professional exchange and development, not as the agency executing an investigation.

6 Crime-prevention and justice capacity

🎓 UNAFEI and UNODC

43:28-51:52

UNAFEI

The lecture introduces UNAFEI as a body concerned with crime prevention and the treatment of offenders. It links the institute with rehabilitation, reformation and capacity building for judges, prosecutors, police officers and policy makers 00:44:42.

Correction: full name and institutional type. UNAFEI is the United Nations Asia and Far East Institute for the Prevention of Crime and the Treatment of Offenders. It is a UN-affiliated regional institute established in 1962 by agreement between the United Nations and the Government of Japan. It is based in Tokyo, currently in Akishima City.

Its place in a criminology answer is capacity rather than enforcement. Training, seminars and research help criminal-justice personnel improve systems for prevention, administration and offender treatment 00:46:27.

UNODC

UNODC is the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime. The teacher gives 1997 as its starting year and Vienna as its headquarters 00:47:49. Drug trafficking receives particular emphasis, but the office is placed within a wider transnational organized-crime and criminal-justice framework.

8 From knowledge to answer

✍️ Decode a past-paper question

01:00:56-01:26:08

The final part of the class is an answer-planning exercise. The teacher's method is deliberately brief: before writing detail, identify what the question wants, who must appear in the answer and how the parts will be organized.

The three filters

1. Demand

What must the answer do: explain mechanisms, analyse contributions, evaluate performance, propose a plan, or combine several tasks?

2. Stakeholders

Which bodies, institutions, victims, justice actors or states are actually relevant to that demand?

3. Outline

In what sequence will the answer define, explain, apply, evaluate and conclude?

The five-minute rule. The lecturer directs students to practise this process on past-paper questions until each demand-stakeholder-outline exercise can be completed within five minutes 01:20:18. This is the planning stage, not the full answer.

Example A: controlling human trafficking

The class takes a question about how international policing and monitoring organizations control human trafficking 01:01:20. The demand is not a generic description of trafficking. It is the mechanisms used by the relevant bodies.

INTERPOL and Europol support law-enforcement cooperation; UNODC supports anti-trafficking policy, technical assistance and criminal justice; UNICEF is relevant where children are affected; the IPA may contribute professional learning but is not an investigative agency.

Example B: international contributions and a plan for Pakistan

A later exercise identifies two separate demands: analyse what UNODC and UNICEF contribute to the criminal-justice system, then propose a strategic plan for Pakistan to use or implement relevant programs 01:23:59.

  1. Introduction: define the international support problem and state the two-part task.
  2. Contribution section: organize UNODC and UNICEF by mandate, mechanism and justice-system effect.
  3. Pakistan diagnosis: identify institutional-capacity or enforcement gaps relevant to the question.
  4. Strategic plan: assign actions to Pakistani institutions and match each proposed partnership to the correct international body.
  5. Conclusion: connect international support with domestic ownership, implementation and accountability.

Reusable answer structure

  1. Decode the command. Circle the verbs and separate multiple demands.
  2. Name the stakeholders. Include only bodies whose mandates address those demands.
  3. Define the framework. Give a short institutional map before detail.
  4. Organize by mechanism. Explain cooperation, information, technical assistance, training, protection or reform as appropriate.
  5. Apply to Pakistan. Identify the domestic partner, institutional gap and route of cooperation.
  6. Use verified support. Add a current initiative, report or case with exact title and date.
  7. Conclude on implementation. International support works through capable, accountable domestic institutions.

Common mistakes

  • Calling a Red Notice an international arrest warrant.
  • Using 1948 as Pakistan's INTERPOL membership year instead of 1952.
  • Giving Europol or the IPA independent arrest and investigative powers.
  • Calling all six bodies UN specialized agencies.
  • Treating UNICEF as a police organization.
  • Listing every organization even when its mandate does not address the demand.
  • Using an unverified child-labour report title, publisher or year.
  • Writing facts before deciding the question's demand and stakeholders.

Editorial: the thesis to carry. International crime control depends on matching the problem with the right institution and mechanism. A strong exam answer demonstrates that match, explains the legal and operational limits, and then applies the framework to Pakistan.
